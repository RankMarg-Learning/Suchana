⚡ Key Highlights — Read This First
  • UPSC has officially released the Civil Services (Prelims) 2026 Admit Card on 18 May 2026 at upsc.gov.in.
  • The Preliminary Examination is scheduled for Sunday, 25 May 2026 across 77 exam centres in India.
  • Admit card is mandatory — no candidate will be permitted inside the hall without a valid e-Admit Card printout.
  • Hall ticket must be accompanied by an original photo ID (Aadhaar, Passport, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving Licence).
  • Candidates must report to the exam centre by 09:00 AM for Paper I (9:30 AM start). Late entry is not allowed.
  • Prohibited items: mobile phones, electronic devices, watches, wallets, and any written material.

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has released the Hall Ticket / e-Admit Card for the Civil Services (Preliminary) Examination 2026. Candidates who successfully completed the online application process between February and April 2026 can now download their admit cards from the official UPSC portal. The examination is scheduled for 25 May 2026 (Sunday).

ℹ️
Official Source
The admit card is available only at the official UPSC website: upsc.gov.in → e-Admit Card section. Do not rely on third-party links or Telegram channels.

⬇️ How to Download UPSC Prelims 2026 Admit Card

01
Visit Official UPSC Website
Open your browser and go to the official UPSC website. Navigate to the "e-Admit Card" section from the main menu or the What's New section on the homepage.
→ upsc.gov.in
02
Click on "Civil Services (Preliminary) Examination 2026"
Find the relevant link titled "e-Admit Card: Civil Services (Preliminary) Examination 2026" and click on it to proceed.
03
Enter Registration Details
Log in using your Registration ID (or Roll Number) and Date of Birth as registered during the application process.
04
Download and Print the Admit Card
Download the admit card as a PDF and print at least 2–3 copies on A4 paper. Use a laser printer if possible. Do not fold the admit card across the photo/barcode area.
⚠️
Important Warning
UPSC does not send admit cards by post. Beware of any website claiming to provide UPSC admit cards other than upsc.gov.in. Do not pay any amount for admit card downloads.

📄 Documents Required at Exam Hall

Candidates must carry both the following on exam day. Entry will be denied if either is missing:

🖨️ Printed e-Admit Card (black & white or colour printout accepted) Mandatory
🪪 Original Photo ID — Aadhaar / Passport / Voter ID / PAN / Driving Licence Mandatory
📸 2 recent passport-size photographs (same as uploaded in application) Recommended
✏️ Ball-point pen (Black/Blue) for OMR sheet. HB pencil for rough work only. Recommended

🚫 Prohibited Items — Read Carefully

The following items are strictly prohibited inside UPSC exam centres. Candidates caught with prohibited items will be debarred:

Category Prohibited Items
Electronic Devices Mobile phones, smartwatches, Bluetooth earphones, calculators, digital watches, cameras
Stationery Correction fluid (whitener), geometric instruments, extra sheets, printed notes
Personal Items Wallets, pouches (only transparent small pouch allowed), food items (except water in transparent bottle)
Apparel Cap, scarf, stole, jacket (unless religious requirement declared in advance)

📝 UPSC Prelims 2026 Exam Pattern

Paper Subject Questions Marks Duration
Paper I General Studies (GS) 100 200 2 Hours
Paper II CSAT (Aptitude) — Qualifying Only 80 200 2 Hours

Paper I (GS) is used for merit ranking and determines who qualifies for Mains. Paper II (CSAT) is only qualifying — candidates must score ≥33% (66 marks out of 200). Negative marking applies: ⅓ mark deducted for each wrong answer in both papers.

✅
Exam Day Checklist
Reach the exam centre at least 30 minutes before your reporting time. Gate closes 10 minutes before exam starts. Read the instructions on the admit card carefully, especially the centre address and room number.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What if my admit card has an error in name or photo?

Immediately contact UPSC through their official helpline or the Facilitation Counter (Ph: 011-23385271/23381125/23098543) on working days between 10 AM–5 PM. Minor discrepancies do not prevent you from appearing — carry your original ID.

Can I appear without an admit card if I show my OTR ID?

No. The physical printed admit card is compulsory. OTR (One-Time Registration) ID alone will not be accepted at the exam centre.

Is there a link to check my exam centre on the map?

Your admit card has the full address of your allotted exam centre. Use Google Maps or the UPSC website to verify the location a day before the exam. UPSC does not provide a separate centre lookup tool.
